# Home Loan Guide -- FastFinance India

## What is a Home Loan?

A home loan is a secured loan where the property being purchased or constructed serves as collateral. Because the loan is backed by an asset, home loans typically offer longer tenures and lower interest rates than unsecured personal loans.

FastFinance India offers home loans for:
- Purchase of a ready-to-move-in flat or house
- Purchase of an under-construction property from a registered developer
- Construction of a new house on owned land
- Extension or major renovation of an existing property (subject to valuation)

Home loans are not available for purchase of commercial property or agricultural land.

## Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

The LTV ratio is the percentage of the property value that FastFinance can lend you. The remaining amount must come from your own funds (down payment).

RBI guidelines cap LTV ratios as follows:
- Properties valued **above Rs. 75 lakhs:** maximum LTV of 75% (you pay at least 25% as down payment)
- Properties valued **between Rs. 30 lakhs and Rs. 75 lakhs:** maximum LTV of 80% (you pay at least 20%)
- Properties valued **up to Rs. 30 lakhs:** maximum LTV of 90% (you pay at least 10%)

**Example:** For a property valued at Rs. 80 lakhs, the maximum loan amount is Rs. 60 lakhs (75% LTV). You must arrange at least Rs. 20 lakhs from your own funds.

## Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR)

FOIR is the percentage of your monthly income that goes toward all loan EMIs combined. FastFinance India uses a FOIR of 50% -- meaning your total monthly EMI obligations (including the proposed home loan EMI and any existing EMIs) must not exceed 50% of your net monthly income.

**Example:** If your net monthly income is Rs. 1,00,000, your total EMI obligations cannot exceed Rs. 50,000. If you already pay Rs. 10,000 per month on a car loan, the maximum home loan EMI FastFinance will approve is Rs. 40,000.

Ask QuickLoan to check whether your income, existing obligations, and proposed loan amount pass the FOIR test.

## Loan Tenure

Home loans at FastFinance India are available for 5 years to 30 years (60 to 360 months).

- Longer tenure = lower EMI but more total interest paid over the life of the loan
- Shorter tenure = higher EMI but significantly lower total interest cost

Most borrowers choose 15-20 years for a balance between manageable EMI and total interest outgo.

## Floating vs Fixed Rate

**Floating rate:** The interest rate moves in line with benchmark rates (RBI repo rate changes). When rates fall, your EMI or remaining tenure decreases. When rates rise, they increase. Most FastFinance home loans are on a floating rate.

**Fixed rate:** The rate is locked in for an initial period (typically 2-5 years), after which it reverts to floating. Useful if you want payment certainty in the short term.

Ask QuickLoan for the current applicable rate for each option.

## Prepayment

For floating-rate home loans, there is no prepayment charge -- you can make part-prepayments or close the loan early at any time without penalty. This is mandated by RBI guidelines for floating-rate retail loans.

For fixed-rate home loans, a prepayment charge may apply during the fixed-rate period. Ask QuickLoan for the specific charge.

## Tax Benefits

Under Section 24(b) of the Income Tax Act, the interest component of your home loan EMI is eligible for a tax deduction. Under Section 80C, the principal repayment component is also eligible for deduction (subject to the overall 80C limit).

Consult your tax advisor for the exact limits applicable to your situation. FastFinance India cannot provide tax advice.

## Required Documents

**For salaried applicants:**
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 6 months' bank statement (salary account)
- Latest Form 16 and ITR (2 years)
- Aadhaar card and PAN card
- Property documents: sale agreement / allotment letter, approved plan, title documents
- Photographs of the property (for valuation)

**For self-employed applicants:**
- Last 2 years' ITR with computation sheets and P&L statement
- Last 12 months' bank statement
- Business proof (GST registration, audited financials)
- Aadhaar card and PAN card
- Property documents as above
