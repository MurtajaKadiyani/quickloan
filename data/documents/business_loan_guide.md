# Business Loan Guide -- FastFinance India

## What is a Business Loan?

A business loan from FastFinance India is an unsecured loan for self-employed individuals and business owners. It provides working capital, funds business expansion, covers equipment purchase, or bridges cash flow gaps -- without requiring you to pledge business assets as collateral.

FastFinance India offers business loans to:
- Sole proprietors
- Partnership firms
- Private limited companies (directors as co-borrowers)
- Individuals with a professional practice (doctors, architects, chartered accountants) running as a business entity
- Traders, manufacturers, and service businesses registered in India

## Eligible Business Types

Business loans are available across most sectors including retail, manufacturing, services, healthcare, education, hospitality, and professional services.

Businesses in sectors with heightened regulatory risk (such as finance, real estate development, and gambling-related activities) are not eligible for FastFinance unsecured business loans.

## CIBIL Score for Business Loans

For business loans, FastFinance India evaluates two CIBIL scores:
1. **Individual CIBIL score** of the proprietor, partner, or director (personal creditworthiness)
2. **CIBIL MSME Rank / Commercial Bureau Score** of the business entity, where available

If the business does not have a commercial credit history, the individual's score carries more weight. A strong personal credit history can compensate for a new business with limited credit track record.

For your applicable rate based on your personal CIBIL score, ask QuickLoan.

## Collateral

FastFinance India's standard business loan product (up to Rs. 25 lakhs) is fully unsecured -- no property, equipment, or other asset needs to be pledged.

For business loan amounts above Rs. 25 lakhs, security may be required depending on the assessment. The loan officer will advise on the type and value of security needed based on the specific application.

## Loan Tenure

Business loans are available for 12 months to 84 months (1 to 7 years).

- Shorter tenures are typically used for working capital requirements
- Longer tenures are used for equipment purchase or larger expansion investments

A business loan's cash flow impact (EMI as a proportion of monthly business revenue) is assessed during underwriting. The tenure selected should be comfortable relative to the business's regular revenue.

## Required Documents

**Individual applicant (proprietor):**
- Last 2 years' ITR with computation sheets and P&L statement
- Last 12 months' bank statement (primary business account)
- Business registration proof (GST registration certificate, Udyam/MSME certificate, shop and establishment licence, or trade licence)
- Aadhaar card and PAN card (individual and business)

**Partnership firm:**
- Partnership deed
- Last 2 years' ITR for the firm and all partners
- Last 12 months' bank statement (firm account)
- GST registration
- PAN card and Aadhaar card of all managing partners

**Private limited company:**
- Certificate of Incorporation and Memorandum of Association
- Last 2 years' audited financial statements
- Last 12 months' bank statement
- Board resolution authorising the loan and naming authorised signatories
- PAN card and Aadhaar card of directors who are co-borrowers

For professional practices (doctors, CAs), additionally: professional registration certificate or licence.
