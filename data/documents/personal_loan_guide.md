# Personal Loan Guide -- FastFinance India

## What is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan -- you do not need to pledge any asset as collateral. FastFinance India lends you a lump sum based on your income, employment stability, and credit history. Because there is no security backing the loan, the eligibility criteria are stricter and interest rates are higher than secured loans such as home loans or gold loans.

The funds are deposited directly into your bank account and you can use them for any legitimate personal purpose.

## Eligible Purposes

Personal loans from FastFinance India can be used for:
- Medical expenses (hospital bills, surgery, rehabilitation)
- Wedding expenses
- Home renovation (not construction of a new property -- that requires a home loan)
- Travel (domestic or international)
- Education fees (course fees, certification costs)
- Debt consolidation (closing high-interest credit card outstanding)

Personal loans cannot be used for speculative investments, gambling, or any activity prohibited by law.

## CIBIL Score and Your Interest Rate

Your CIBIL score (credit score from 300 to 900) directly affects the interest rate you are offered. A higher score signals lower credit risk and qualifies you for a lower rate. A lower score means higher risk and a higher rate -- or possible rejection.

**Why does CIBIL matter?**
It summarises your repayment history on all previous credit products: credit cards, loans, EMIs. Late payments, defaults, and high credit card utilisation lower your score. Consistent on-time repayments over time improve it.

For your personalised interest rate based on your specific CIBIL score, ask QuickLoan -- it will look up the current rate applicable to your score band from the FastFinance rate table.

## Loan Tenure

Personal loans at FastFinance India are available for 12 months to 60 months (1 to 5 years).

- **Shorter tenure:** Higher monthly EMI, but lower total interest paid over the life of the loan.
- **Longer tenure:** Lower monthly EMI, but more interest paid overall.

Ask QuickLoan to calculate EMI for different tenure options to help you decide.

## EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)

Your EMI is the fixed amount you pay each month. It includes:
- A portion that repays the principal (the amount borrowed)
- A portion that pays the interest for that month

In the early months of a loan, a larger share of your EMI goes toward interest. As the loan progresses, the principal repayment share increases (amortisation schedule). Ask QuickLoan to calculate your EMI for a given loan amount and tenure.

## Prepayment

Prepayment is permitted after you have completed 12 full EMI payments. A prepayment charge applies on the outstanding principal at the time of prepayment. Full foreclosure (closing the loan entirely before the end of tenure) is also available after 12 months, subject to the same charge.

The specific prepayment charge percentage is in your loan agreement -- ask QuickLoan for the current figure based on your loan product.

## Required Documents

**For salaried applicants:**
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 6 months' bank statement (salary account)
- Latest Form 16 or ITR
- Aadhaar card (identity and address proof)
- PAN card

**For self-employed applicants:**
- Last 2 years' Income Tax Returns (ITR) with computation sheets
- Last 12 months' bank statement (business account)
- Business registration proof (GST registration, Udyam certificate, or trade licence)
- Aadhaar card and PAN card
