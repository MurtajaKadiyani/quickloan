# Frequently Asked Questions -- FastFinance India

---

**What is a CIBIL score and why does it matter?**
CIBIL (Credit Information Bureau India Limited) is India's largest credit bureau. Your CIBIL score (300-900) summarises your repayment history on all credit products -- loans, EMIs, credit cards. Lenders use it to assess how likely you are to repay a new loan on time. A higher score qualifies you for a lower interest rate and increases your chances of approval. A score below a product's minimum threshold results in rejection regardless of income.

---

**How can I improve my CIBIL score?**
Pay all EMIs and credit card dues on time -- this has the biggest impact. Reduce your credit card outstanding to below 30% of your credit limit. Avoid applying for multiple loans simultaneously (each application creates a credit enquiry that temporarily lowers your score). Review your credit report for errors and dispute them if found. Improvement takes 6-12 months of consistent on-time payment.

---

**What is the difference between pre-qualification and final loan approval?**
Pre-qualification is QuickLoan's initial check: based on the information you share (income, CIBIL score, loan amount), it tells you whether you likely meet the eligibility criteria. It is not a loan offer or a guarantee. Final approval requires document verification by a FastFinance loan officer, a formal credit assessment, and (for home loans) a property valuation. Pre-qualification is step one; approval is step three.

---

**Can self-employed individuals apply for a FastFinance loan?**
Yes. Personal loans, home loans, business loans, and gold loans are all available to self-employed individuals. The documentation is different from salaried applicants -- you will need ITR (last 2 years), bank statements, and business registration proof instead of salary slips. Ask QuickLoan which documents you need for your specific product and business type.

---

**What happens if my loan application is rejected?**
FastFinance India will inform you of the rejection and provide the primary reason (insufficient income, low CIBIL score, FOIR exceeded, etc.). You can reapply after addressing the reason for rejection -- typically 3-6 months for CIBIL score improvement or after increasing income. Avoid applying again immediately, as repeated applications lower your CIBIL score.

---

**What is an EMI and how is it calculated?**
EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed monthly amount you pay for a loan. It is calculated based on the loan amount, the interest rate, and the tenure using a standard amortisation formula. In the early months, a larger share of the EMI goes toward interest; over time, the principal repayment share increases. For your specific EMI amount, ask QuickLoan -- it will calculate it from the database based on your loan amount, your applicable rate (from your CIBIL score), and your chosen tenure.

---

**What is a processing fee?**
A processing fee is a one-time charge deducted at the time of loan disbursal to cover the cost of application processing, document verification, and credit assessment. It is expressed as a percentage of the loan amount. For the current processing fee applicable to each product, ask QuickLoan.

---

**What is foreclosure?**
Foreclosure means repaying the entire outstanding loan principal before the end of the original tenure. For floating-rate home loans, foreclosure is free (no penalty) under RBI guidelines. For personal loans and business loans (fixed rate), a foreclosure charge applies after 12 EMIs. Ask QuickLoan for the specific charge for your product.

---

**How long does loan approval take?**
- **Gold loan:** Same day (requires branch visit for gold appraisal)
- **Personal loan:** 2-5 business days after document submission
- **Business loan:** 5-10 business days after document submission and business assessment
- **Home loan:** 10-20 business days after document submission and property valuation

Timelines depend on the completeness of your documents and the speed of the property valuation process (for home loans).

---

**What documents do I need?**
Documents vary by loan product. In general: Aadhaar card (identity + address proof), PAN card, proof of income (salary slips or ITR), bank statements, and for home loans, property documents. For gold loans, only Aadhaar and PAN are needed -- no income proof. Ask QuickLoan for the full document list for your specific product and employment type.

---

**Can I apply for two loans simultaneously?**
Yes, provided your combined FOIR (total monthly EMIs ÷ net monthly income) stays within 50% for personal/home loans and you meet each product's individual eligibility criteria. Applying for two loans at the same time results in two credit enquiries, which may temporarily lower your CIBIL score. If you need both loans, it is better to stagger applications by 3-6 months where possible.

---

**What is the maximum loan amount I can get?**
Maximum amounts vary by product: personal loan up to Rs. 5 lakhs, home loan up to Rs. 1 crore, business loan up to Rs. 25 lakhs (unsecured), gold loan depends on your gold's assessed value (75% LTV). Final approved amount also depends on income, FOIR, and CIBIL score. Ask QuickLoan with your specific income and CIBIL score for a more accurate maximum estimate.

---

**What is a balance transfer?**
A balance transfer allows you to move an existing loan from another lender to FastFinance India at a potentially lower interest rate. The outstanding principal of your current loan is paid off by FastFinance, and you continue repaying FastFinance at the new rate. This is most commonly used for personal loans and home loans where a significant outstanding balance remains. Eligibility and rate depend on your current loan status and CIBIL score.

---

**What is QuickLoan and what can it not do?**
QuickLoan is FastFinance India's AI pre-qualification assistant. It can tell you whether you appear to meet eligibility criteria, calculate EMI, explain product features and documentation requirements, and provide applicable interest rates from the FastFinance rate table.

QuickLoan cannot: approve your loan, access your actual CIBIL score, verify your income or documents, or guarantee a final loan amount or rate. Final approval always requires a formal application reviewed by a FastFinance loan officer.

**Can I repay my gold loan early?**
Yes. Gold loans can be closed at any time by repaying the outstanding principal and accrued interest. There is no minimum tenure requirement for gold loans -- you can close the loan the next day if you wish, and your gold is released immediately upon full payment. Early closure charges do not apply to gold loans.

---

**What happens if gold prices fall after I take a gold loan?**
The loan amount is fixed at the time of pledge, based on the gold rate on that day. If gold prices fall significantly during the loan tenure, FastFinance India may issue a margin call -- requesting you to either make a partial repayment to bring the outstanding principal back within the 75% LTV limit, or to pledge additional gold. This is a standard industry practice governed by RBI guidelines. You will be notified in advance if a margin call is triggered.
