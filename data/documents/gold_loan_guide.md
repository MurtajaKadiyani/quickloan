# Gold Loan Guide -- FastFinance India

## What is a Gold Loan?

A gold loan is a secured loan where you pledge gold ornaments or jewellery as collateral and receive a loan amount based on the gold's assessed value. The gold is held safely at a FastFinance branch vault and returned to you when the loan is fully repaid.

Gold loans are one of the fastest forms of credit available -- in most cases, disbursal happens on the same day you visit the branch with your gold.

## Why Choose a Gold Loan?

- **No CIBIL score required:** Unlike personal or business loans, gold loans do not depend on your credit history. The gold itself is the security.
- **Minimal income requirement:** No income documentation is required for a gold loan -- you do not need to provide salary slips or ITR. However, a minimum monthly income of Rs. 10,000 applies. FastFinance verifies this through a self-declaration at application; no income documents need to be submitted.
- **Same-day disbursal:** Faster than any unsecured loan, which typically involves multi-day document review.
- **Lower interest rate than a personal loan:** Because the loan is secured by an asset, the risk to the lender is lower and rates are typically lower than personal loans.

## Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

RBI guidelines cap gold loan LTV at 75% of the gold's assessed value. This means:

- If your gold is valued at Rs. 1,00,000, the maximum loan amount FastFinance can offer is Rs. 75,000
- FastFinance India's internal assessment is based on the prevailing gold rate at the time of pledging, applied to the net weight of pure gold content (after adjusting for alloy in the ornament)

The gold rate used for valuation is the rate on the day of pledge -- not a historical rate.

## Gold Purity Requirement

Only gold ornaments or jewellery with a purity of **18 carats or higher** are accepted for a gold loan. Coins, bars, and gold with a purity below 18 carats are not accepted.

Gold is assessed by a certified appraiser at the branch. The weight, purity, and assessed value are recorded and provided to you in writing before the loan is disbursed.

## Repayment Options

Gold loans at FastFinance India offer two repayment structures:

**EMI-based repayment:** Pay equal monthly instalments comprising principal and interest, over the chosen tenure. This is similar to a standard loan EMI.

**Bullet repayment:** Pay only the interest each month (or in advance at the time of disbursement), and repay the full principal at the end of the tenure. This keeps monthly cash outflow very low. Suitable if you expect a lump sum at a specific future date (salary bonus, property sale proceeds, etc.)

Ask QuickLoan which repayment structure suits your cash flow situation.

## What Happens if the Loan is Not Repaid?

This is important to understand before taking a gold loan.

If you fail to repay the loan and interest by the end of the tenure (or by the due date for EMI payments), FastFinance India will send formal notices to your registered address and mobile number. If repayment is still not made after the notice period:

**FastFinance India is legally entitled to auction the pledged gold** to recover the outstanding loan and interest. The auction proceeds are applied to the outstanding amount, and any surplus (after recovering the loan, interest, and auction costs) is returned to you.

To avoid losing your gold, contact the branch proactively if you are facing difficulty making payments -- loan restructuring options may be available.

## Gold Storage and Safety

Your pledged gold is:
- Stored in the branch's secured vault (fireproof and monitored)
- Insured against theft, fire, and natural calamities for the full assessed value for the duration of the loan
- Returned to you in the same condition it was pledged, at the time of full repayment

You will receive a pledge receipt at the time of depositing the gold. Keep this safe -- it is required for gold release at the time of loan closure.

## Required Documents

Gold loans have the lightest documentation of any FastFinance product:
- Aadhaar card (identity and address proof)
- PAN card (for loans above Rs. 50,000 -- PAN is mandatory)
- The gold ornaments/jewellery (physically brought to the branch)
- Passport-sized photograph

No bank statements or business documents are required. Income is verified by self-declaration only (no salary slips or ITR needed).
